About Me

I am running for 4EVER Knights President because I care deeply about this organization, the ambassadors who make it meaningful, and the future we have the opportunity to build. Through my time on the executive board as Vice President of Ambassador Connections, I have learned how much intentional leadership, communication, organization, and follow-through shape the ambassador experience.

Over the past year, I have worked closely with ambassadors, officers, advisors, and campus partners. Those experiences have helped me understand not only what 4EK does well, but also where we have room to grow. I do not want to reinvent 4EK. I want to strengthen it, clarify its identity, and make sure every ambassador feels informed, supported, connected, and proud to represent this organization.

My Vision

My vision is centered on elevating 4EK. I want 4EVER Knights to be recognized not only for the events we support, but for the leadership, service, professionalism, and community our ambassadors bring to UCF.

One moment that shaped this vision was being asked at an event, “What does 4EK do?” I could explain that we support events, but when asked what makes 4EK uniquely 4EK — our signature initiatives, traditions, and identity — I realized we have an incredible organization, but we can do more to clearly define and document who we are.

As president, I want to help build that clarity. That means strengthening our traditions, improving documentation, developing stronger systems, expanding our campus presence, and creating experiences that help ambassadors understand the mission of 4EK and feel proud to be part of it.

Election Responses

Below are my detailed responses to the election questions. My video introduces my overall vision, while this section provides more specific information about my qualifications, leadership style, and goals.

Why are you interested in running for President?

I am interested in running for President because I believe 4EK has an incredible foundation, and I want to help strengthen it for both current and future ambassadors. Through my time on the executive board, I have seen how much potential this organization has to support ambassadors, build community, and represent UCF. I am running because I want to elevate 4EK by clarifying our identity, improving our systems, investing in our ambassadors, and expanding our presence across campus.

What qualifications or experiences make you prepared for this role?

My experience as Vice President of Ambassador Connections has prepared me to understand the responsibilities, challenges, and opportunities of executive board leadership. I have worked on communication, planning, ambassador engagement, professional development, and collaboration with other officers and advisors. I have also developed retreat plans and budgets, supported organizational projects, and gained additional leadership experience through my involvement in other campus roles. These experiences have helped me build the communication, organization, and follow-through needed to serve as President.

What new ideas or initiatives would you bring to 4EK?

My main initiatives are focused on elevating 4EK’s identity, ambassador experience, organization, and campus presence. I would like to create stronger documentation and transition materials, expand the mentor/mentee program, improve communication and transparency, strengthen recruitment partnerships with KoRT and Housing, and build more intentional Fall and Spring retreat experiences. I also want to continue investing in professional development, alumni engagement, and opportunities that help ambassadors grow.

How would you describe your leadership style?

My leadership style is collaborative, organized, and intentional. I believe in listening to people before making decisions, but I also understand that leadership requires confidence, fairness, and follow-through. I want ambassadors to feel comfortable coming to me with ideas or concerns, while also knowing that I will make decisions based on what is best for the organization as a whole.

How would you improve communication and transparency?

I would improve communication by making expectations, reminders, and important updates more consistent and easier to access. I would like to explore regular updates such as a President’s Corner, clearer reminders before meetings and events, and more centralized resources so ambassadors do not have to search for information. Transparency is important because ambassadors should understand not only what decisions are being made, but why they are being made.

How would you support the executive board and officers?

I would support the executive board by maintaining regular communication, helping officers set realistic goals, and ensuring they have the resources and clarity needed to succeed. I would also prioritize officer check-ins, collaboration, and strong transition materials so each officer feels supported in their role. A strong president should not do everything alone; they should help the whole board work effectively together.

How would you approach recruitment?

I would work closely with Recruitment from the beginning to help prepare for the upcoming cycle, especially because we may be starting later than ideal. I want to use existing infrastructure more effectively by working with KoRT for tabling and involvement opportunities and with Housing for flyer distribution and residence hall outreach. I would also like to make better use of the recruitment materials already in 4EK storage while improving our tabling presence as budget allows.

How would you approach Fall and Spring retreats?

I would make Fall and Spring retreats distinct but connected. Fall Retreat would focus on welcoming ambassadors, building community, introducing traditions, and launching the mentor/mentee program through a larger reveal and team challenges. Spring Retreat would be a chance to welcome any new ambassadors, reconnect returning members, reflect on the mission of 4EK, and boost morale midway through the year. I have already developed Spring Retreat planning and budgeting materials, so I would be able to build from that existing work.

How would you improve the mentor/mentee program?

I would like to make the mentor/mentee program more intentional from the start. During orientation, ambassadors could complete mentor/mentee forms so pairings are ready for a larger reveal at Fall Retreat. Retreat could include mentor/mentee challenges that help groups bond and practice teamwork early. Throughout the semester, I would also like either the President or VP to check in with mentors, see how they are doing, and remind them to connect with their mentees. This would make the program feel more active and supported rather than simply assigning pairs and hoping they stay connected.

How would you represent 4EK to outside groups?

I would represent 4EK by communicating clearly who we are, what we do, and why our work matters. I want 4EK to have a stronger platform and a clearer identity across campus. That means strengthening partnerships, improving recruitment materials, documenting our signature initiatives, and making sure ambassadors can confidently explain the value of 4EK. My goal is for 4EK to be recognized as a meaningful leadership organization with a strong presence at UCF.
